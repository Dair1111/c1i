A TALE BEGUN

The world is never ready
for the birth of a child.
Our ships are not yet back from wineless voyages.
Our swords are not yet forged
for fresh injustices.
Our cities are not yet built
for new deportations.
We still lack songs,
to drown out the shrieks.

The child was already born,
it's just that they had no time to find a place for hope,
that they postponed happiness.
They had no room, and that room was taken
for pain.

When the child is born,
the family says a prayer to time,
beseeches patience.

They whisper to the tree outside the window,
"Grow with him, tree."

The child cries and cries
and the wind blows.
